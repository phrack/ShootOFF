ShootOFF

An open source framework to enhance laser dry fire training.

Welcome to Project ShootOFF!

Tired of perfecting your shooting skills by repeatedly pulling a trigger on a gun loaded with snap caps? With ShootOFF and a laser trainer you can turn your dry fire practice into a realistic, effective, and fun training experience! ShootOFF turns your home into a safe, clean shooting range you can use any time you want.

ShootOFF is free, open source software that enhances laser dry fire training by detecting your shots using a computer and a commodity webcam. Beyond detecting shots, you can enable one of our default training protocols or install new ones to cause ShootOFF to perform interesting operations with your shots. Training protocols range from games intended to keep laser dry firing fun to focused training exercises that make commonly recommended training practices more real and interactive.

ShootOFF also supports projectors (a second monitor can be used instead) which enables the use of animated targets and more realistic training than is possible using only paper targets.

ShootOFF is intended to be used with laser cartridges sold by companies like Laser Ammo and LaserLyte and laser trainers such as SIRT sold by NextLevel Training. A more complete list of hardware for laser dry fire training is available on our wiki here.

This project was started in response to the large number of expensive, unreliable, and primitive hardware products sold online to act as targets for laser dry fire training. Why buy new hardware when you can use hardware you already own with free software?

Features

  • ShootOFF supports multiple cameras so that you can set up a full virtual shoot house whereever you are or to simply use one camera for yourself and another for a friend during competitions.
  • Virtual targets you can overlay on top of physical targets you've hung on your wall that are visible on your webcam feed.
    • Virtual targets are used by training protocols to detect exactly where your shot landed on the physical target.
  • The target editor helps you create virtual targets for any target you print out to use.
    • ShootOFF comes with a number of targets that are in the public domain or where we have been granted permission to use a target that is subject to a copyright. With the target editor you can use any target you want: don't be limited by our current selection!
  • Rich configuration options allow you to use ShootOFF with nearly any laser trainer in most reasonable lighting conditions. Our default settings are sufficient for most trainers and lighting conditions out of the box.
  • Using a projector or a second monitor allows you to use animated targets and more realistic training protocols.
  • Training protocols make dry fire training fun and more effective.
  • The configurable virtual magazine helps you practice reloads while you dry fire.
  • Practice malfunction drills by turning on malfunctions and configuring the rate at which they occur.
  • Our software is free as in no cost and free as in freedom: all of ShootOFF's code is provided free of charge to do anything you want with it as long as you comply with the GPL.

Download

The current stable version of ShootOFF is version 3.0, see what's new.

Regardless of the operating system you are using, ensure you have the latest version of Java installed. Download the ZIP by clicking the link on the menu to the left, extract its contents, and double click ShootOFF.jar. Linux users may need to use the command line instead: java -jar ShootOFF.jar.

Documentation and Troubleshooting

We work hard to ensure that our wiki is constantly up to date with the latest information for using, configuring, and troubleshooting the current version of ShootOFF. Please turn to the FAQ if you have any questions. If you have a suggestion or a problem that isn't addressed on the wiki please either join us on Reddit, email project.shootoff@gmail.com, or open a new issue on our GitHub issue tracker.

Contributing

We are always willing to accept code contributions on our GitHub repository. Please feel free to email project.shootoff@gmail.com or open an issue to discuss a feature you'd like to or are implementing.

If you do not know how to write software, we are always looking for help in the art department (icons, graphics for the program and website, etc.). We can also always use help in advertising ShootOFF: tell a friend about the project, help a relative set-up ShootOFF, or post about your experience with the project on your favorite forum!

We do not accept monetary donations at this time. We suggest donating to Project Appleseed or your local civil rights organization instead. If you make a donation out of gratitude for this project, please make us feel good by letting us know!
